Graph

Since the purpose of using a visualization is to explore data, discover patterns and relations, and eventually build knowledge, many visualization systems encode the userâ€™s interactions as knowledge graphs, concept graphs, or history graphs. In most cases, the nodes in these graphs represent a data item (e.g., a document, a location, an entity), an abstract concept (e.g., user annotations), or a visualization state (e.g., user histories). Edges then represent connections or relations between these entities. In this section, we identify publications that that use a Graph-based encoding approach and group them into two categories: Entity and Concept Graphs and History Graphs.
